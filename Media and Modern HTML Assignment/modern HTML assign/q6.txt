1. header:
   the <header> element represents the introductory content at the beginning of a section or a page. It typically contains the page's logo, navigation menu, headings, or other introductory elements.

2. nav:
    The <nav> element represents a navigation menu or links to other pages or sections of the website.

3. main:
    The <main> element represents the main content of the page, excluding headers, footers, sidebars, and navigation.

4. article:
     The <article> element represents a self-contained piece of content that can be distributed and reused independently, such as a blog post, article, or news story.
     
5. footer:
       The <footer> element represents the footer of a section or a page. It typically contains copyright information, contact details, site maps, or links to related pages.
